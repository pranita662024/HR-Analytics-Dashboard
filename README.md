# HR-Analytics-Dashboard

**📌 Project Overview**
This project presents an interactive HR Analytics Dashboard developed using Power BI to analyze employee attrition and workforce trends. The dashboard helps HR professionals identify key factors influencing employee turnover and supports data-driven decision-making through interactive visualizations and KPIs.

**🎯 Business Problem**
Organizations often struggle to understand the underlying causes of employee attrition, leading to increased recruitment costs, productivity loss, and workforce instability.

This dashboard addresses challenges such as:
1. High employee attrition
2. Increased recruitment and training costs
3. Productivity gaps
4. Lack of visibility into attrition drivers
5. Workforce engagement challenges
6. Need for data-driven HR decisions

**🎯 Project Objectives**
1. Analyze employee attrition patterns.
2. Monitor key HR performance indicators.
3. Identify departments and employee groups with higher turnover.
4. Understand the impact of age, salary, tenure, overtime, and business travel.
5. Provide actionable business recommendations to improve employee retention.
   
**🛠 Tools & Technologies**
1. Power BI Desktop
2. Power Query
3. DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)
4. Data Modeling
5. Data Visualization
   
**📊 Dashboard Pages**

1️⃣ Executive Overview
Provides an overview of key HR metrics, including total employees, attrition rate, average salary, and workforce demographics.

2️⃣ Job & Tenure Analysis
Examines employee attrition based on job role, department, salary, business travel, and years at the company.

3️⃣ Risk & Behaviour Analysis
Analyzes employee behavior, including overtime, commuting distance, previous companies, and job level to identify retention risks.

**📈 Key KPIs**
1. Total Employees
2. Active Employees
3. Attrition Count
4. Attrition Rate
5. Average Salary
6. Average Age

**💡 Key Business Insights**
Employees aged 26–35 show the highest attrition.
Research & Development has the highest employee turnover.
Employees in lower salary bands are more likely to leave.
Overtime is associated with increased attrition.
Employees with shorter tenure experience higher turnover.
Business travel and job level influence employee retention.

**✅ Business Recommendations**
1. Strengthen onboarding and mentorship programs.
2. Review compensation for lower salary bands.
3. Reduce excessive overtime to improve work-life balance.
4. Focus retention efforts on high-attrition departments.
5. Provide career development opportunities for early-career employees.
6. Monitor HR metrics regularly using interactive dashboards.
